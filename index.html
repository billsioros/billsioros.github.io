GPA: 8.49 out of 10

Carried out my BSc thesis “Generating realistic nanorough surfaces via a Generative Adversarial Network” under the supervision of Dr. G.Giannakopoulos and Dr. V.Constantoudis.

We focused on how a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) based approach, given a nanorough surface data set, can learn to produce statistically equivalent samples. Additionally, we examined how pairing our model with a set of nanorough similarity metrics, can improve the realisticity of the resulting nanorough surfaces. We showcased via multiple experiments that our framework is able to produce sufficiently realistic nanorough surfaces, in many cases indistinguishable from real data.

GPA: 9 out of 10
I’m currently working on my thesis wherein we explore the adaptation of prompt-guided techniques from large-scale language-image models to the audio domain, particularly applying them to autoregressive text-to-audio models for the first time. Leveraging the Prompt-to-Prompt concept, originally used in image editing, we expand their functionality for intuitive audio manipulation. Users can edit audio content based on textual prompts without necessitating model modifications, retraining, or additional data. Our innovative approach ensures high fidelity and intuitive editing, granting unprecedented control over audio generation. This research pioneers the application of these techniques to autoregressive text-to-audio models, showing promise for breakthroughs in AI-driven audio synthesis and creative expression.
